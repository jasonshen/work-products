# Founder Mental Health Research Landscape

*What's been studied, what's missing, and where a YC-community survey could break new ground.*

*Compiled March 17, 2026*

---

## The Big Picture

Founder mental health research is a young field dominated by a single landmark study (Freeman et al., 2015/2018) that almost every subsequent piece of reporting cites. Beyond that, there are a handful of surveys from startup organizations, some academic work on specific traits (ADHD, Dark Triad), and almost nothing on several dimensions you care about (autism prevalence, childhood trauma, medication usage, coaching/therapy uptake). The field is ripe for a comprehensive, well-designed survey.

## STUDY-BY-STUDY BREAKDOWN

### 1. Freeman et al. — "Are Entrepreneurs Touched with Fire?" (2015) → expanded as "The Prevalence and Co-occurrence of Psychiatric Conditions Among Entrepreneurs and Their Families" (2018)

🔗 [Pre-print (2015)](https://michaelafreemanmd.com/Research_files/Are%20Entrepreneurs%20Touched%20with%20Fire%20(pre-pub%20n)%204-17-15.pdf) | [Peer-reviewed (2018, Small Business Economics)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11187-018-0059-8) | [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/325089478)

**The foundational study. Everything else references this.**

- **Researchers:** Michael Freeman (UCSF psychiatrist), Paige J. Lerner, Richard Hunt (Virginia Tech), and others
- **Published:** Pre-print 2015; peer-reviewed in *Small Business Economics*, 2018
- **Sample:** 242 entrepreneurs + 93 comparison participants (non-entrepreneurs)
- **Geography:** US only
- **Method:** Online survey using validated screening instruments (PHQ for depression, ASRS for ADHD, etc.)

**Key findings:**
- 49% of entrepreneurs reported at least one lifetime mental health condition
- Depression: 30% (vs. 15% comparison)
- ADHD: 29% (vs. 5% comparison — **6x higher**)
- Substance use: 12%
- Bipolar: 11% (vs. 1% comparison — **10x higher**)
- 72% had mental health concerns when including family history
- Entrepreneurs were significantly more likely to have first-degree relatives with mental health conditions

**Strengths:** Validated instruments, comparison group, published in a respected journal
**Weaknesses:**
- Small sample (n=242)
- Self-selected (recruited via entrepreneur networks — survivorship bias)
- US only
- No autism/ASD measurement
- No childhood trauma/ACEs measurement
- No Dark Triad measurement
- No data on treatment-seeking (therapy, coaching, medication)
- Now 10+ years old

### 2. Startup Snapshot — "The Untold Toll" Report (2023)

🔗 [Full report](https://www.startupsnapshot.com/research/the-untold-toll-the-impact-of-stress-on-the-well-being-of-startup-founders-and-ceos/) | [Forbes coverage](https://www.forbes.com/sites/annefield/2023/04/29/startup-founders-report-entrepreneurship-is-taking-a-toll-on-their-mental-health/)

- **Researchers:** Yael Benjamin and team, with Intel Ignite, Econa, Arnon-Tadmor Levy, Zell Entrepreneurship Program
- **Sample:** 400+ early-stage startup founders and CEOs
- **Geography:** International (Israel-weighted, but multi-country)
- **Method:** Online survey

**Key findings:**
- 72% of founders report entrepreneurship has impacted their mental health
- 37% suffer from anxiety
- 54% very stressed about the future of their startup
- 81% keep fears and struggles to themselves
- Only 23% see a psychologist or coach
- Barriers: cost (73%), lack of time (52%)

**Strengths:** Decent sample size, international, asked about treatment barriers
**Weaknesses:**
- Not peer-reviewed
- No validated psychiatric instruments (self-report of general categories)
- No ADHD, autism, bipolar, or substance use breakdowns
- No childhood trauma
- No Dark Triad
- No medication usage data
- Likely skewed toward Israeli/European tech ecosystem

## THE 23% STAT: SOURCE AND CONTEXT

The widely-cited claim that "only 23% of founders seek help from a psychologist or coach" comes from **Startup Snapshot's "The Untold Toll" report (April 2023)**. It surveyed 400+ early-stage founders internationally (Israel-weighted). The exact finding:

> "Only 23% have sought help or have seen a psychologist to discuss the issues facing them. Eighty-one percent report they do not openly share their stress, fears, and challenges."

**Important context:** This stat is often cited alongside CEREVITY's finding that "only 23% of founders seek mental health support" — but that appears to be the same Startup Snapshot number re-cited, not an independent confirmation. CEREVITY's own survey (n=127, California) seems to reference the Startup Snapshot figure.

**Ironic coincidence:** Gallup found in 2023 that **23% of ALL U.S. adults** visited a mental health professional in the past 12 months 🔗 [Gallup](https://news.gallup.com/poll/467303/americans-reported-mental-health-new-low-seek-help.aspx) (KFF confirmed: 23% of adults received mental health treatment in 2022, up from 19% in 2019 🔗 [KFF](https://www.kff.org/mental-health/the-share-of-young-adults-who-received-mental-health-treatment-jumped-45-from-2019-to-2022-the-largest-increase-among-any-age-group/)). So founders' help-seeking rate appears roughly equal to the general population — despite reporting significantly higher rates of mental health conditions.

**What's missing from the 23%:** The Startup Snapshot survey lumped "psychologist or coach" into a single question. It did NOT separately measure:
- Therapy (psychologist, therapist, counselor)
- Executive coaching
- Psychiatry (medication management)
- Peer groups / founder support groups
- Apps (Headspace, BetterHelp, etc.)
- Medication without therapy
- Informal support (friends, partners, mentors)

The barriers they DID capture: cost (73%), lack of time (52%).

## "ENTREPRENEURS GENERALLY" vs. "STARTUP FOUNDERS" — A Critical Distinction

Most research conflates these two very different populations. Here's what we know about each:

### General Entrepreneurs / Self-Employed / Small Business Owners

This is a much broader category — includes freelancers, shop owners, consultants, restaurateurs, sole proprietors. The research here is larger but less relevant to YC-style tech founders.

**Key studies:**

**Stephan, Rauch & Hatak (2023) — "Happy Entrepreneurs? Everywhere?"**
🔗 [Sage Journals](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/10422587211072799) | [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/358141018)
- **Type:** Meta-analysis of 94 studies, 319 effect sizes, 82 countries
- **Published:** *Entrepreneurship Theory and Practice*
- **Key finding:** Entrepreneurs report **higher** positive wellbeing (life satisfaction, work satisfaction) than employees — but ALSO higher stress. The "autonomy paradox": more freedom, more anxiety.
- **Nuance:** This is mostly measuring small business owners, not venture-backed founders. The autonomy benefits are strongest for lifestyle entrepreneurs, not growth-stage startup founders.

**Jamal (1997, 2007) — Self-employed vs. salaried employees**
- Found self-employed reported **higher emotional strain and poorer mental health** than salaried employees
- One of the earlier studies making this comparison

**Reid et al. (2018)**
- Self-employment is **positively associated with short-term psychological distress and depression**

**Wolfe & Patel (2019)**
- Entrepreneurs experience more stress-related health issues than employees

**Inc.com / National Small Business Association survey (2023)**
🔗 [Inc.com article](https://www.inc.com/jeff-haden/a-new-study-says-75-percent-of-entrepreneurs-are-concerned-about-their-mental-health.html)
- 75% of small business owners concerned about mental health
- **56% diagnosed** with anxiety, depression, or stress-related problems by a doctor
- This is a MUCH higher diagnosis rate than Freeman's 49% — likely because it includes stress-related diagnoses (a broader category)

**Simply Business UK — "Mind Your Business" (2023-2024)**
🔗 [2024 report](https://www.simplybusiness.co.uk/knowledge/general/small-business-mental-health-report/) | [Campaign page](https://www.simplybusiness.co.uk/campaign/mind-your-business-2/) | [2023 data](https://www.simplybusiness.co.uk/knowledge/articles/2023/05/data-reveals-self-employed-mental-health-struggles/)
- n=~2,000 UK small business owners
- 56% experienced poor mental health in past 12 months (2023), improved to 47% (2024)
- Anxiety: 59%, Depression: 38%, Loneliness: 22%

**Australian Chamber of Commerce (ACCI) National Business WHS Survey (2024)**
🔗 [CCIWA coverage](https://cciwa.com/business-pulse/34-of-small-business-owners-diagnosed-with-mental-health-issues-survey/)
- 34% of Australian business owners had a diagnosed mental health condition
- Lower than US/UK figures — may reflect cultural differences in diagnosis rates

**French entrepreneurs study (Small Business Economics, 2025)**
🔗 [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11187-025-01087-2)
- Four surveys of French small business owners
- Focused on daily recovery experiences (detachment, relaxation)
- Found entrepreneurs have worse recovery patterns than employees

**German self-employed study (Frontiers in Psychology, 2024)**
🔗 [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1365489/full)
- n=117 self-employed in Germany
- Pilot study on stress and coping mechanisms
- Small sample, narrow focus

**Cardon et al. (2024) — "The Many Faces of Entrepreneurial Loneliness"**
🔗 [Wiley / Personnel Psychology](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/peps.12614)
- Published in *Personnel Psychology* (top-tier journal)
- Used Reddit data + surveys
- Found loneliness drives exhaustion, burnout, sleep problems, anxiety, depression, even suicidal ideation
- Entrepreneurs rated loneliness 7.6/10

### What's DIFFERENT About Startup Founders

The "general entrepreneur" research has a fundamental problem when applied to VC-backed tech founders:

| Dimension | General Entrepreneur | Startup Founder |
|-----------|---------------------|-----------------|
| **Scale of stakes** | Personal savings, maybe a loan | Millions in VC capital, fiduciary duty |
| **Growth pressure** | Lifestyle business is fine | 10x-or-die expectations |
| **Identity fusion** | "I own a business" | "I AM the company" |
| **Social comparison** | Local competitors | Twitter/X culture of performative success |
| **Exit timeline** | Open-ended | 7-10 year clock on investor returns |
| **Team dynamics** | Often solo or small | Cofounder conflict, board dynamics, hiring/firing |
| **Failure mode** | Business closes quietly | Public, documented, searchable forever |
| **Selection effects** | Mixed motivations | Disproportionately risk-seeking, novelty-seeking |
| **Age/stage** | Often 40s-50s | Often 20s-30s (YC median) |
| **Autonomy** | Usually high (the upside) | Increasingly constrained by investors, board, team |

**The meta-analyses showing entrepreneurs are "happier than employees" are almost entirely driven by lifestyle/self-employed entrepreneurs who chose autonomy.** Venture-backed founders are a fundamentally different population with different stressors, and the research barely distinguishes between them.

**Studies that DO focus on startup founders specifically:**
1. Startup Snapshot (2023) — 400+ early-stage founders
2. CEREVITY (2025) — 127 California tech founders
3. Sifted (2024) — 156 European founders
4. Endeavor Brazil (2024) — 118 tech founders

**That's it.** Four studies, all small, none peer-reviewed, none using validated psychiatric instruments. Everything else either uses Freeman's 2015 data (which recruited via entrepreneur networks, not startup-specific) or general entrepreneur/self-employed samples.

## TOPIC-BY-TOPIC ANALYSIS

### ADHD ✅ Well-studied (relatively)

**Best coverage of any condition.** Freeman found 29% prevalence (6x general population). Johan Wiklund (Syracuse University) has built an entire research program around ADHD and entrepreneurship.

**Key academic work:**
- Wiklund, Yu, Tucker, Marino (2017): "ADHD, Impulsivity and Entrepreneurship" — *Journal of Business Venturing*. 🔗 [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0883902616302348)
- Wiklund et al. (2016): Qualitative study of 14 Swedish entrepreneurs with ADHD diagnoses. 🔗 [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/305769751)
- Yu, Wiklund, Pérez-Luño (2021): "ADHD Symptoms, Entrepreneurial Orientation and Firm Performance" — *ETP*. 🔗 [Sage](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/1042258719892987)
- Tran, Wiklund et al. (2025): Meta-analytical review of ADHD and entrepreneurship. 🔗 [Sage](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/10422587251392498)
- Freeman, Lerner, Rauch (2024): "Dopamine and Entrepreneurship" — *JBVI*. 🔗 [RePEc](https://ideas.repec.org/a/kap/sbusec/v53y2019i2d10.1007_s11187-018-0059-8.html)

**Gap:** All academic work studies ADHD → entrepreneurship direction. No large-scale prevalence study within a specific founder cohort (e.g., YC). No data on diagnosis timing (before vs. after founding), medication usage patterns, or how ADHD interacts with other conditions in founders specifically.

---

### Depression ✅ Covered, but surface-level

Freeman found 30% prevalence. Startup Snapshot found 72% report mental health impact (with depression as a component). Multiple surveys confirm elevated rates.

**Gap:** No studies distinguish between clinical depression (MDD), situational depression (reactive to startup failure/stress), and dysthymia (chronic low-grade). No data on antidepressant usage rates among founders. No longitudinal data (does depression precede founding, or does founding trigger it?).

---

### Anxiety ✅ Covered, but blurred with stress

Multiple surveys confirm anxiety as the #1 reported condition (37-50%+ depending on survey). But most surveys don't distinguish between:
- Generalized Anxiety Disorder (clinical)
- Situational/startup-related anxiety
- Performance anxiety
- Social anxiety

**Gap:** No validated GAD-7 or similar instrument used in founder-specific surveys. Anxiety is typically lumped with "stress" and "burnout."

---

### Autism / ASD ❌ Almost completely unstudied in founders

**This is a major gap.**

- Freeman's study did NOT measure autism/ASD
- No founder-specific study has measured autism prevalence
- The Barclays/Entrepreneurs Network "Neurodiverse Founders" report (2024, UK) 🔗 [Barclays Eagle Labs](https://labs.uk.barclays/learning-and-insights/news-and-insights/thought-leadership/neurodiverse-founders/) | [Sifted coverage](https://sifted.eu/articles/neurodiverse-founders-report-2024) surveyed 500 neurodiverse founders — but only included people who *already identified* as neurodiverse, so it measured discrimination, not prevalence
- Anecdotal/cultural evidence (e.g., r/ycombinator thread "Why do neurodiverse startup founders seem to do so well?") suggests high autism traits in tech founders, but no quantitative data exists
- Peter Thiel's famous "slightly autistic" comment about successful founders remains unsubstantiated by research

**Opportunity:** A YC survey could be the first to measure autism traits (using AQ-10 or similar) in a large, defined founder cohort. This alone would be publishable.

---

### Childhood Trauma / ACEs ⚠️ Studied in general entrepreneurship, NOT in startup founders specifically

**Academic work exists, but not founder-specific:**
- "What does not kill you makes you stronger" (JBR, 2022) 🔗 [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0148296322005732) | [Full PDF](https://midus.wisc.edu/findings/pdfs/2573.pdf)
- "Childhood adversities: Mixed blessings for entrepreneurial entry" (JBV, 2023) 🔗 [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0883902623000010)
- "Childhood trauma and entrepreneurs' individual entrepreneurial orientation" (JSBM, 2024) 🔗 [Taylor & Francis](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/00472778.2024.2309653)

**The gap:**
- No study has measured ACE scores specifically in tech/startup founders
- No study has looked at how ACEs interact with ADHD, depression, or entrepreneurial outcomes
- No data comparing founder ACE scores to general population
- The "entrepreneurship as trauma response" thesis (Kelly Campbell, Entrepreneur.com) is popular but has no quantitative backing

**Opportunity:** Including a brief ACE screener in a YC survey would be first-of-its-kind data for the tech founder population.

---

### Dark Triad (Narcissism, Machiavellianism, Psychopathy) ⚠️ Studied academically, but NOT prevalence-measured in real founders

**Academic work (mostly on entrepreneurial *intention*, not actual founders):**
- Hmieleski & Lerner (2016): "The Dark Triad and Nascent Entrepreneurship" — *JSBM*. 🔗 [Taylor & Francis](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1111/jsbm.12296) | [PDF](https://www.hmieleski.com/Publications/Hmieleski_Lerner_JSBM_2016.pdf)
- Zhao et al. (2022): Dark Triad → entrepreneurial intentions via opportunity recognition. 🔗 [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S2352673422000087)
- Cross-country comparison (2024): Narcissism → entrepreneurial intentions. 🔗 [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11365-024-01001-4)
- "The bright side of a dark personality" (JBR, 2024). 🔗 [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0148296324005484)

**The gap:**
- Almost all studies use *student samples* measuring *intention* to start a business — not actual startup founders
- No study has measured Dark Triad prevalence in a defined founder cohort
- No data on how Dark Triad traits correlate with founder mental health conditions, treatment-seeking, or outcomes
- The ACEs → Dark Triad link is well-established in psychology 🔗 [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10875951/) but has never been studied in a founder population

**Opportunity:** Measuring Dark Triad traits (using Short Dark Triad / SD3 — 27 items) alongside ACEs and mental health conditions in actual founders would be genuinely novel.

---

### Executive Coaching / Therapy / Psychiatrist Usage ❌ Barely studied

**What exists:**
- Startup Snapshot: 23% of founders see a psychologist or coach; barriers are cost (73%) and time (52%)
- CEREVITY: 23% seek mental health support (same stat widely cited)
- Forbes (2023): Anecdotal reporting from "over a dozen CEOs plus a half-dozen psychologists, coaches and venture capitalists"
- No survey has distinguished between: therapy, executive coaching, psychiatry, peer groups, meditation apps, medication

**The gap:**
- No data on WHAT founders use (therapy vs. coaching vs. medication vs. peer groups)
- No data on HOW MUCH they use it (frequency, duration, spending)
- No data on PERCEIVED EFFECTIVENESS of different modalities
- No data on medication types (SSRIs, stimulants, anxiolytics, etc.) or usage rates
- No data on whether founders differentiate between coaching and therapy, or use both

**Opportunity:** This is arguably the most actionable gap. A survey that maps treatment modalities to conditions and perceived effectiveness would be immediately useful to YC, to coaches, to VCs designing founder support programs, and to the mental health industry targeting founders.

## WHAT A YC SURVEY COULD UNIQUELY OFFER

### 1. First autism/ASD prevalence data in a defined tech founder cohort
No one has done this. Using the AQ-10 (10 items) or AQ-28 would be groundbreaking.

### 2. First ACE score data in tech/startup founders
The "entrepreneurship as trauma response" narrative is everywhere but has zero data behind it for this population.

### 3. First Dark Triad measurement in actual founders (not students)
Every existing study uses undergrads or MBA students measuring *intention*. Measuring actual YC founders would be a first.

### 4. Treatment modality mapping
What founders actually use, how often, what they find effective. Therapy vs. coaching vs. medication vs. peer groups vs. nothing. This doesn't exist anywhere.

### 5. Interaction effects
ADHD × childhood trauma × Dark Triad × treatment-seeking — these interactions have never been studied together in any population, let alone founders. Even preliminary correlational data would be publishable.

### 6. Defined, homogeneous cohort
YC founders are a natural study population: shared accelerator experience, similar stage, similar pressures, large enough N (~5,000+ alumni) for statistical power. Previous studies suffer from vague "entrepreneur" definitions that include everything from freelancers to Fortune 500 CEOs.

### 7. Longitudinal potential
If the survey is designed well, it can be re-administered annually to track how mental health changes across company stages.

## METHODOLOGICAL NOTES FOR SURVEY DESIGN

**Validated instruments to consider:**
- **ADHD:** ASRS-v1.1 (6-item screener) or full 18-item
- **Autism:** AQ-10 (10 items) — quickest validated screener
- **Depression:** PHQ-9 (9 items) — gold standard
- **Anxiety:** GAD-7 (7 items)
- **Childhood Trauma:** ACE-R (revised, 14 items) or brief ACE (10 items)
- **Dark Triad:** Short Dark Triad (SD3, 27 items) or Dirty Dozen (12 items — faster but less reliable)
- **Substance Use:** AUDIT-C (3 items for alcohol) + DAST-10 (10 items for drugs)
- **Burnout:** MBI (22 items) or single-item burnout measure

**Total validated items: ~80-100 (plus demographics and treatment questions)**
**Estimated completion time: 15-20 minutes**

**Key design decisions:**
- Screener-level vs. diagnostic-level instruments (screeners are appropriate for surveys)
- Anonymous vs. confidential (anonymous increases honesty but prevents longitudinal tracking)
- Incentive structure (YC community response rates are likely high with endorsement from YC partners)
- IRB approval if aiming for academic publication
- Comparison group: could use population norms for most validated instruments, or recruit a matched non-founder sample

## BOTTOM LINE

The field has one good prevalence study (Freeman, 2015/2018) that's now a decade old with n=242. Everything else is either:
- Small samples with no validated instruments (Startup Snapshot, Sifted, CEREVITY)
- Academic studies using student samples, not actual founders (Dark Triad work)
- Single-condition studies that don't look at interactions (Wiklund's ADHD work)
- Non-US populations that aren't generalizable to YC (Endeavor Brazil)

A well-designed YC survey with validated instruments across all seven dimensions (ADHD, autism, depression, anxiety, childhood trauma, Dark Triad, treatment usage) would immediately become the most comprehensive study of founder mental health ever conducted. With ~500+ responses from a defined cohort, it would be publishable in a top entrepreneurship journal and citeable for a decade.
