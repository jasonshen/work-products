# Transformative Experiences at Men's Retreats: A Research Report

*Compiled from first-person accounts across Reddit, personal blogs, Medium, GQ, Condé Nast Traveller, Substack, and retreat organization testimonials. March 2026.*

---

## Executive Summary

This report documents 27 specific activities, exercises, and experiences that men describe as powerful and transformative at outdoor-focused men's retreats. The research draws from first-person accounts across ManKind Project (New Warrior Training Adventure), EVRYMAN, Sacred Sons, Men Without Masks, and independent wilderness retreats. Several clear patterns emerge: the most transformative experiences combine **physical intensity or discomfort** with **emotional vulnerability**, often in **nature settings** that strip away everyday identity markers.

---

## The Experiences

### 1. Sacred Combat / Boxing Circle
**Organization:** Sacred Sons  
**What it is:** One man steps into a circle with an intention (often tied to a personal wound), calls another man to meet him, and they fight with boxing gloves — not to win, but as a form of energetic exchange and emotional release.

> *"I always thought I was a pacifistic person, but actually I like fighting! I like it almost a scary amount… What surprised me was how much I enjoyed it."*  
> — Charles Eisenstein, author & speaker, Sacred Sons Convergence

> *"My neck still hurts from my fight with my brother Kale. But it is good hurt, it reminds me of why I fought."*  
> — Personal blog, Sacred Sons Immersion Amsterdam

**Why it works:** Controlled physical confrontation accesses embodied rage and fear that talk-based work can't reach. The ritual container (intention-setting, witnessing circle, facilitator guidance) transforms violence into vulnerability.

---

### 2. Primal Sound / Vocal Release
**Organization:** Sacred Sons  
**What it is:** One man enters a circle and produces raw, wordless sound — not singing, not screaming, just pure vocal expression of whatever emotion is present. Another man witnesses and holds space.

> *"I caught myself evading eye-contact with the brother in the circle… I reminded myself that I was here to be brave. For me, it would have been most easy to just tap into some primal rage and roar like an ape. But that would not have been true for me, because I did not feel any such raw power. Just anxiety. What sound belongs to that?"*  
> — Samhildánach blog, Sacred Sons Amsterdam

**Why it works:** Bypasses the verbal/intellectual defenses men are trained to hide behind. Forces connection to emotion through the body rather than the mind.

---

### 3. The Opening Circle / First Share
**Organization:** Multiple (Men Without Masks, EVRYMAN, Sacred Sons)  
**What it is:** On arrival, before any bonding or trust has been built, men sit in a circle and share why they're there — often prompted with deeply personal questions about masks, shadows, and what they're hiding.

> *"When it was my turn, I heard myself talking in a small voice, about being 40, and a bit lost… Stuff that has lingered from the pandemic and left me feeling stuck. I was surprised that my story sounded true; doubly surprised that it felt so good to say it to a series of faces that suddenly looked a bit warmer, and a bit more human."*  
> — Condé Nast Traveller journalist, Men Without Masks

> *"Before any personal introduction of the participants, we were already sharing very personal stories with brothers-yet-to-meet. We were asked the most personal of questions. About masks and what you're hiding in the shadows."*  
> — Samhildánach blog, Sacred Sons

**Why it works:** Vulnerability before comfort. Sharing before trust is established forces men past the usual slow-warming social contract. The relief of being heard without judgment is immediate and disarming.

---

### 4. Morning Healing Circle with Facilitated Emotional Process
**Organization:** Sacred Sons  
**What it is:** Small circle of ~20 men. Facilitators guide men who step forward through deep emotional work — accessing grief, rage, shame, joy. Often involves physical release (being held down while expressing rage, collapsing into sobs, being supported by other men).

> *"Man after man accessed deep grief, rage, shame, brokenness, forgiveness, joy, or peace. There weren't just tears, there were wracking sobs that came from the depths of the soul. There weren't just shouts; there were screams of rage that came from generations of pain."*  
> — Charles Eisenstein, Sacred Sons Convergence

> *"At least half had been physically or sexually abused. One man held his daughter on his chest as she died. Another suffered years of an invisible chronic disease… Several times I thought, 'If this man can heal, every man can heal.'"*  
> — Charles Eisenstein

**Why it works:** Skilled facilitation + the "field of love" created by other men witnessing without judgment. The physical holding (literal restraint while a man rages) creates paradoxical safety — you can fully let go because you're being contained.

---

### 5. Physical Restraint + Emotional Release ("Being Held Down")
**Organization:** Sacred Sons  
**What it is:** Seven or more men physically hold a man to the ground while he works through deep rage or grief. Facilitators guide the process, encouraging him to follow the energy.

> *"I'm one of seven men holding down another as these words escape his throat in a guttural roar. He struggles against our weight, veins bulging along his neck, eyes brimmed with tears… 'You're safe, brother,' whispers the main facilitator. 'Follow the energy. Where does it want to go? Don't use words this time.' The man roars again, raw powerful rage, before bursting into heaving sobs."*  
> — Ian MacKenzie, Medium, Sacred Sons Convergence

**Why it works:** Paradox of safety through containment. The body needs to feel held before it can fully release. Multiple men providing physical contact while emotional release happens creates both safety and brotherhood simultaneously.

---

### 6. Sweat Lodge Ceremony
**Organization:** ManKind Project, Men Without Masks, various  
**What it is:** A small, dark, sealed structure heated with lava stones. Men sit shoulder to shoulder for extended periods in extreme heat, often with guided prayers, chanting, or sharing. Usually done in rounds.

> *"I'd never done a sweat lodge… for me that was the most physically challenging part, but we all supported each other."*  
> — Andrew Noske, ManKind Project NWTA

> *"A cramped sweat lodge overseen by a shaman"* combined with days of other work led to profound breakthroughs.  
> — Condé Nast Traveller, Men Without Masks

**Why it works:** Extreme physical discomfort (heat, claustrophobia, darkness) strips away pretense. You can't maintain your social mask when you're fighting to breathe. Shared suffering bonds the group. The womb-like darkness creates psychological regression to a more open state.

---

### 7. The "Hundred Club" (Rage Release with Baseball Bat)
**Organization:** Men Without Masks  
**What it is:** 100 savage hits with a baseball bat on a prone punching bag. Other men play the role of the participant's parents or key figures while he releases controlled anger.

> *"I watched the scowling guy from day one change, especially while doing the 'Hundred Club'… as other men played the part of the parents who had abused or neglected him. Afterwards, he lay sobbing in the foetal position, then embraced his parents, telling them he loved them, before being literally lifted up by the group — a moment that seemed to fill the room with magic electricity."*  
> — Condé Nast Traveller, Men Without Masks

**Why it works:** Physical exhaustion breaks through emotional armor. Role-playing parents/abusers while hitting creates psychodramatic catharsis. Being physically lifted by the group afterward provides the corrective relational experience — "these men will catch you."

---

### 8. Cold Water Immersion / Dawn Plunge
**Organization:** Men Without Masks, various  
**What it is:** Early morning (often 6:30 AM) group immersion in natural cold water — pond, river, or ocean — with intention-setting beforehand.

> *"An intention-setting 6:30am frigid dip in a pond in a beautiful corner of the estate"*  
> — Condé Nast Traveller, Men Without Masks

**Why it works:** Cold water is the great equalizer. No amount of status, money, or social skill helps you in freezing water. It resets the nervous system, creates shared physical challenge, and the post-immersion euphoria bonds the group. Morning timing sets a tone: "we do hard things here."

### 9. Intense Breathwork Leading to Altered States
**Organization:** Men Without Masks, EVRYMAN, Sacred Sons  
**What it is:** Extended sessions of intentionally irregular, intense breathing (similar to holotropic breathwork), often combined with dynamic meditation and movement, leading to non-ordinary states of consciousness.

> *"After a long session of intense, intentionally irregular breathing, followed by dynamic meditations… it was at that moment that Craig White implored us to let our loved ones in, and something unfurled deep inside me. Suddenly, I felt as though I was enveloped in white light, and could feel everyone I'd ever cared about come thudding into my chest."*  
> — Condé Nast Traveller, Men Without Masks

**Why it works:** Breathwork bypasses cognitive defenses by directly altering nervous system state. The combination with emotional prompts (inviting loved ones in) at the moment of maximum openness creates powerful emotional breakthroughs without requiring "talk therapy" skills.

---

### 10. Wilderness Solo Sit (Solo Time in Nature)
**Organization:** Outward Bound, Cascadia Quest, Men's Therapy Online, various  
**What it is:** Each man is sent out alone into nature — no phone, no journal, no distractions — for hours or sometimes days. Just sitting with whatever arises.

> *"Within minutes, the mind starts to rebel. You'll want to fidget, fix something, do something. But if you stay through the boredom, the restlessness, and the stories in your head, something starts to shift. You begin to feel your breath again, you notice the wind in the trees, and the slow rhythm of your own heartbeat."*  
> — Men's Therapy Online, "The Burdens We Carry" retreat

> *"Being oneself in a more authentic way, feeling more serene and peaceful, and securing a purpose in life comprised the psychospiritual transformations participants experienced."*  
> — Academic study, Frontiers in Psychology

**Why it works:** Boredom is the gateway drug to self-awareness. Without distraction, the things you've been running from surface. Nature provides a non-judgmental mirror. The simplicity strips away identity markers and social performance.

---

### 11. Noble Silence (Extended Period Without Speaking)
**Organization:** Men Without Masks  
**What it is:** Nearly a full day spent in silence, often combined with solo time in nature.

> *"There was almost an entire day of 'noble silence' in nature, which for me involved meditating barefoot in a river and later terrifying a group of unsuspecting dog-walkers as I blissfully exited a forest covered in mud."*  
> — Condé Nast Traveller, Men Without Masks

**Why it works:** Removes the primary tool men use to manage social situations (talking, joking, intellectualizing). Forces presence. In combination with nature immersion, creates conditions for spontaneous, unstructured self-discovery.

---

### 12. Rhythmic Chest Beating / Group Drumming
**Organization:** Sacred Sons  
**What it is:** Men rhythmically beat their own chests, accelerating together until the room erupts in howling.

> *"Jeddy Azuma stands shirtless, leading nearly 100 men as we rhythmically beat our chests. He accelerates the chest beating and we unconsciously follow suit, creating a faster and faster beat until Azuma lets out a howl with such startling force that his dreads shake. With zero hesitation, the other men join him, culminating in a wolfishly distorted chorus."*  
> — GQ, Sacred Sons online convergence

**Why it works:** Rhythmic physical action induces group trance states. The howling releases inhibition. The synchronized movement creates tribal bonding without requiring emotional disclosure — it's brotherhood through shared embodiment.

---

### 13. The Shadow Walk (Solo Night Walk in Darkness)
**Organization:** Men's Therapy Online  
**What it is:** Each man walks solo through woods at night — no flashlight, no talking. Following a marked but dark path.

> *"Your heart will race. Your mind will play tricks. Every snap of a twig feels like a test. Every moment you want to turn back becomes a mirror. This isn't about pushing past fear — it's about meeting it."*  
> — Men's Therapy Online, "The Burdens We Carry" retreat

**Why it works:** Confronting literal darkness as metaphor for internal darkness. Physical fear (alone in woods at night) accesses the same nervous system response as emotional fear, creating a bridge between body and psyche.

---

### 14. The Severance Ritual (Mountain Release)
**Organization:** Men's Therapy Online  
**What it is:** Group hike to a mountain peak. Each man carries a physical object representing something he's ready to release. At the summit, men speak what needs to die, witnessed by the group.

> *"What needs to die? What are you done carrying? There's no performance here. No bullshit. Just raw truth shouted into the sky, witnessed by men who've earned the right to hear it."*  
> — Men's Therapy Online, "The Burdens We Carry" retreat

**Why it works:** Physical effort (the hike) creates embodied investment. The object creates tangible connection to abstract emotional burden. The height and expansive view provide perspective. The witnessing by men who've shared the climb together creates earned intimacy.

---

### 15. Mass Vocal Release / Group Ululation
**Organization:** ManKind Project  
**What it is:** Large group collectively screaming, ululating, or vocalizing emotions simultaneously.

> *"I'd already lost my voice after the mass Ululate Your Feelings assembly, and I'd already had like three or four nervous breakdowns by that point, so Screaming was ultimately the most appropriate prefix for my Jackalopiness."*  
> — Thumper Forge (Patheos blog), ManKind Project NWTA

**Why it works:** Group vocalization creates permission through unanimity. When everyone is screaming, no individual feels exposed. The physical act of screaming releases tension held in the throat, chest, and diaphragm — where men commonly store suppressed emotion.

---

### 16. Visualization / "Through the Cave" Exercise
**Organization:** ManKind Project  
**What it is:** Guided visualization exercises where men journey through symbolic landscapes (caves, villages) and encounter symbolic objects or figures that become personal totems.

> *"For me what I saw was a mossy stone owl, and thus my name became 'solid owl', then eventually changed to 'magnificent owl'. I imagined a village of playful, friendly, deeply present people."*  
> — Andrew Noske, ManKind Project NWTA

**Why it works:** Visualization accesses the subconscious through imagery rather than logic. Receiving a "warrior name" from the process creates a new identity anchor that can persist after the retreat ends.

---

### 17. Shadow Work / Shadow Exercise
**Organization:** ManKind Project  
**What it is:** Structured exercise exploring your "shadow self" — the parts of yourself you hide, deny, or project onto others. Men name their shadow's patterns and how it creates a diminished life.

> *"My shadow creates a world of: dull, mediocre relationships by distractions and playing everything safe… 'You'll be 60 one day and nobody will really know you — just your persona.'"*  
> — Andrew Noske, ManKind Project NWTA

**Why it works:** Naming the shadow defangs it. The Jungian framework gives men an intellectual handle on emotional patterns. The group witnessing prevents the shadow from retreating back into hiding.

---

### 18. Life Mission Statement Creation
**Organization:** ManKind Project  
**What it is:** Guided process to distill a personal mission statement in the format: "I create a world of [X] by [Y]."

> *"I discovered my personal mission and it is this that has brought about the biggest life changes for me."*  
> — Ben Gold, ManKind Project UK testimonial

> *"I create a world of: joyful, deep, juicy relationships by: deep presence and radiant confidence."*  
> — Andrew Noske, ManKind Project NWTA

**Why it works:** Gives men a portable, actionable takeaway from an immersive experience. The format is simple enough to remember but deep enough to guide real decisions. Created in an emotionally open state, it tends to be more authentic than goals set in ordinary consciousness.

---

### 19. "Dropping In" / Somatic Emotional Awareness
**Organization:** EVRYMAN  
**What it is:** Guided practice of dropping from the head into the body — relaxing, opening, and naming what emotions are physically present using five primary emotions (anger, joy, shame, sadness, fear).

> *"Going into our bodies, feeling our emotions and expressing ourselves isn't woo woo. It's human. Each of us has tremendous intelligence and wisdom if we allow ourselves to become extremely present, go deep inside ourselves, listen and express."*  
> — Steve Schlafman, EVRYMAN MELT retreat

**Why it works:** Provides a simple, learnable framework (ROC: Relax, Open, Connect + five emotions) that men can use long after the retreat ends. Reframes emotional awareness from weakness to "intelligence and wisdom."

### 20. Ecstatic Dancing
**Organization:** Men Without Masks  
**What it is:** Free-form, unstructured dancing — often men who would never dance in any other context.

> *"The unlikely ecstatic dancing"* was cited alongside breathwork and meditation as one of the breakthrough experiences.  
> — Condé Nast Traveller, Men Without Masks

**Why it works:** Nothing demolishes masculine social armor faster than dancing without skill or purpose. The absurdity is the point — it breaks the internal critic. Moving without rules reconnects men to spontaneous embodied expression.

---

### 21. Group Physical Lifting / Being Carried by the Group
**Organization:** Men Without Masks, Sacred Sons  
**What it is:** After an emotional breakthrough, the man is literally lifted up by the group — held aloft or cradled.

> *"He was literally lifted up by the group — a moment that seemed to fill the room with magic electricity. For the rest of the retreat, he seemed lighter."*  
> — Condé Nast Traveller, Men Without Masks

**Why it works:** Physical metaphor made real. Being supported, held, and elevated by other men provides a corrective emotional experience — especially for men whose formative experience was that other men are competitors, not supporters.

---

### 22. Electronics/Phone Confiscation
**Organization:** ManKind Project, Men Without Masks, most retreats  
**What it is:** All phones, electronics, reading material, and sometimes personal items are surrendered at check-in.

> *"All other food, electronics and even reading material was taken off us. We were not allowed to return to our cars. I liked that."*  
> — Andrew Noske, ManKind Project NWTA

**Why it works:** Removes the escape hatch. Without the phone, there's no scrolling through discomfort, no texting someone safe, no checking out. Forces full immersion in whatever emotional terrain arises.

---

### 23. Firelight Counsel Circle (Nightly)
**Organization:** Multiple  
**What it is:** Nightly gathering around a fire. Men speak from the gut, listen without fixing, hold each other accountable. Everything welcome, nothing off-limits.

> *"We discussed erections in school showers and the crushing loneliness of empty one night stands; absent fathers, addicted mothers, abused partners; all the guilt and secret shame that we hold in, because there isn't always an outlet for it."*  
> — Condé Nast Traveller, Men Without Masks

**Why it works:** Fire is humanity's original gathering technology. The flickering light makes eye contact easier (less harsh than fluorescent rooms). The darkness beyond the circle creates a sealed container. Speaking taboo subjects around a fire at night feels ancient and safe in a way that a conference room never could.

---

### 24. Survival Challenge / Minimal Gear Wilderness Experience
**Organization:** ManCraft (Wild Heart)  
**What it is:** Men are stripped to swimwear and allowed one survival item (knife or paracord). They work together to find shelter, water, fire, and food.

> *"Wearing only swimmers and taking only a blanket and one other survival item each, we will work together to find shelter, water, fire and food on the landscape."*  
> — ManCraft retreat description

**Why it works:** Strips away all status markers (clothes, possessions, roles). Interdependence becomes mandatory. The shared challenge of basic survival creates bonds that can't be replicated by talking exercises.

---

### 25. Vision Quest / Solo Fast in Wilderness
**Organization:** Rites of Passage / Wilderness Quest (since 1977), Nature Philosophy, Cascadia Quest  
**What it is:** 3-4 days alone in wilderness, fasting on water only. No food, no shelter beyond what you carry, no human contact. Structured as release of old life → threshold crossing → return reborn.

> *"A modern enactment of an ancient ceremony… enables men to engage in a sacred journey in three stages: release of an old life, stepping across the threshold of sacred time and space, and return to the world reborn."*  
> — Wilderness Quest, Rites of Passage

**Why it works:** The most extreme version of the solo sit. Fasting strips the body to essentials and creates altered consciousness naturally. Extended solitude forces confrontation with self. The three-stage structure (separation, threshold, return) mirrors every hero's journey and every genuine transformation.

---

### 26. The Threshold Ritual (Closing Ceremony)
**Organization:** Men's Therapy Online  
**What it is:** Final morning ritual. Men cross a symbolic threshold — often a physical marker on the ground — stepping from who they were into who they're becoming. Witnessed by the group.

> *"The final morning is quieter than the others. There's a different weight in the air — not heavy, but grounded. Men move slower. More deliberately. They're not the same men who arrived on day one."*  
> — Men's Therapy Online, "The Burdens We Carry" retreat

**Why it works:** Ritual closure prevents the "it was just a weekend" dismissal. The physical act of crossing a line creates somatic memory. Being witnessed while doing it makes it a social contract, not just a private intention.

---

### 27. Integration Groups / Ongoing Brotherhood
**Organization:** ManKind Project (I-Groups), EVRYMAN (weekly groups)  
**What it is:** Ongoing weekly or monthly men's circles that continue after the retreat — small groups (6-10 men) who meet regularly to practice the skills learned.

> *"Before the weekend I was fearful, insecure, and isolated. The weekend provided a space where I could meet my biggest fear: Being around men! From that experience, seeds were planted and I know if it had not been for the NWTA, I would not be standing in a place today respecting who I am, engaging with my world and enjoying the company of other men."*  
> — Michael W, ManKind Project UK testimonial

> *"MKP provided a place where the potential for change was available — and the support I needed to help that change happen has been freely available in ways I could never have imagined."*  
> — Rod B, ManKind Project UK testimonial

**Why it works:** The retreat is the catalyst; the integration group is the container for lasting change. Without ongoing community, breakthroughs fade. Weekly practice normalizes vulnerability and emotional expression, making them skills rather than one-time events.

## Cross-Cutting Themes

### What Makes These Experiences Work

1. **Body before mind.** The most impactful experiences bypass verbal/intellectual processing and go straight to the body — hitting, breathing, screaming, freezing, sweating, dancing. Men are trained to think their way through problems; these experiences short-circuit that reflex.

2. **Earned vulnerability.** Physical challenge (cold water, survival, exhaustion) creates a state where emotional walls naturally lower. The vulnerability feels earned rather than demanded.

3. **Witnessing without fixing.** The consistent rule across all these retreats: listen without advice-giving. For men conditioned to solve, being allowed to just *be seen* is revelatory.

4. **Nature as container.** Wilderness strips away status, role, and routine. Fire, water, darkness, and silence do psychological work that no facilitator could replicate indoors.

5. **Ritual structure.** Beginning, middle, end. Threshold crossings. Naming ceremonies. These aren't decorative — they give the brain a framework to process transformation as real rather than temporary.

6. **Brotherhood as medicine.** Across every account, the most commonly cited surprise is: *other men were not what I expected.* The healing isn't just from the exercises — it's from discovering that men can be tender, honest, and safe.

### Notable Critiques

- **Cultural appropriation concerns** around use of sweat lodges, the word "aho," and "traditional tribal" framing (ManKind Project especially)
- **Facilitator readiness** — Sacred Sons has faced criticism for taking men "too deep too fast" without properly trained facilitators
- **Leadership accountability** — Sacred Sons co-founder Aubert Bastiat was accused of misconduct with female clients in 2024
- **Trust violations** — at least one ManKind Project participant reported being lied to about what personal items would be confiscated, creating trust damage that undermined the entire weekend
- **Post-retreat integration** — several accounts note the experience was powerful but didn't translate to lasting change without ongoing community/practice
